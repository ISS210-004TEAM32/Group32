Sharing personal information

Scroll down to read more and learn tips

Posted by Lily Schuehrer on April 28, 2023

It's important to educate children about what's okay to be shared online and what's not. You should never share your full name, family members names, city you live in, address, school you go to, or passwords to other private information. Strangers online can use these pieces of information to hack accounts, steal your identity, blackmail you, or put your life in danger. Monitoring children closely is important to avoid these issues. It's common for online predators to target children or teens who might not know better. They might personally message a child pretending to be someone else to trick them for information. It's also important to look at what websites your child visits as some of them allow children to talk to strangers or access content without parental consent.

Tips on protecting your child and call to action

Taking action is important in combating these security threats. Its recommended parents watch their child's screen time usage throughout the day, monitor the websites and apps they have access to, have an open conversation about what's appropriate to see and what's not appropriate, and give them steps to take if they feel their online safety is being compromised
